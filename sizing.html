How to Measure

Follow these simple steps to ensure accurate measurements for the perfect fit

1

Height

Measure without shoes, from top of head to floor

2

Chest

Measure around fullest part with arms at sides

3

Waist

Measure at natural waistline (where they normally wear pants)

4

Inseam

Measure from crotch to bottom of ankle (for pants/skirts)

Measurement Tips

  • Use a flexible measuring tape
  • Measure over light clothing or underwear
  • Keep tape snug but not tight
  • For accuracy, measure twice
  • Have the child stand naturally with arms at sides

Uniform Fit Guide

How your uniform should fit for maximum comfort and appearance

Shirts & Blouses

Shoulder seams should sit at the edge of the shoulder

Sleeves should end at the wrist bone

Shirt length should cover the waistband of pants/skirt

Pants & Skirts

Waist should fit comfortably without needing to tighten belt excessively

Pants should break slightly at the shoe without dragging

Skirts should fall at regulation length (typically just above the knee)

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about sizing and fit

We recommend sizing up if your child is between sizes, especially for growing children. Our uniforms are designed with some room for growth, and it's better to have a slightly larger uniform that can be adjusted with belts or tailoring than one that's too small.

We recommend allowing about 1-2 inches in length for growing room. For width, the uniform should fit comfortably now with just a little room (about 1 inch) in the chest and waist. Remember that our uniforms are designed to last an entire school year with normal growth patterns.
